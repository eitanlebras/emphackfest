When it rains,
salmon feel it.

When it rains, water washes over the ground and picks up pollutants like oil, pesticides, and trash. This polluted runoff flows into storm drains, which often lead directly to nearby streams and rivers. As a result, the toxic runoff can harm salmon by contaminating their habitat, reducing water quality, and affecting their food sources. By understanding this connection, we can take steps to reduce our impact and help protect salmon populations.

This website is designed to help you understand how your neighborhood contributes to this problem and what you can do to help. By entering your address, you can see how water flows from your location to nearby salmon habitats and learn about the specific impacts of stormwater runoff in your area. Together, we can make a difference for our local salmon populations and the health of our waterways.
